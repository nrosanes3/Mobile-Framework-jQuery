Calm is a Superpower

The Power of Meditation

Do you sometimes feel like you can't remember what you did just 10 minutes ago? Do you often find yourself feeling anxious or impatient? Feel like you lack deep connections with people in your life? What if I told you that there was something you can do, anytime, anywhere that can help you solve all of this?

This practice is called meditation and I'm gonna to tell you what it means, how it can benefit you, and what you can do to get started today.

For thousands of years people have practiced meditation for spiritual, emotional, and physical well-being. Most associate meditation with a monk sitting quietly for hours on top of a mountain. Zen vibes. but in reality it's something anyone can do and anyone can benefit from.

What is Meditation?

Meditation is the essence of awareness - not trying to turn off your thoughts or feelings but simply observing them without judgement. It's being mindful and having the ability to be present, engaged in what we're doing in the moment here and now. It's a skill that needs consistent practice and it takes time to get comfortable.

Benefits of Meditation

So you're probably wondering what exactly can I get from it?

Health

Well, in terms of health, Meditation is a great holistic approach to improve your health and even treat some illnesses. Clinical studies have shown that it can help with insomnia, social anxiety and stress, high blood pressure, depression, and many more.

Focus

It can also help you get better focus and concentration. If you find yourself doing multiple things at once but never actually finishing anything, or if you have a bad habit of procrastination, brain scans have shown an increase in gray matter in the parts of the brain responsible for learning, memory, and emotional regulation.

Connect Better

Speaking of emotional regulation, meditation can increase positive feelings and actions toward yourself and others. It begins with developing kind thoughts and feelings towards yourself and extending this kindness and forgiveness to friends, acquaintances, and even enemies. It has an ability to increase people's compassion toward themselves and others.

Conclusion

The good thing is that you can't fail at meditation. The goal is not to have a completely quiet mind but to notice the thoughts you're having. There are many positive side effects to meditation such as improving your health, focus, and relationship with yourself and others. There are also many guided meditation apps, videos, podcasts, and books that can help you get started.

Lastly, it's meditation practice, not meditation perfect. Sometimes your focus will wander and that's okay. What's important is that you get started and you keep practicing consistently. Take a few minutes after this presentation to do absolutely nothing and begin your meditation journey towards mental clarity.

Types of Meditation

Experienced meditators agree: a daily meditation practice can have significant benefits for mental and physical health. But one thing they probably won't agree on? The most effective types of meditation. That's simply because it's different for everyone. After all, there are literally hundreds of meditation techniques encompassing practices from different traditions, cultures, spiritual disciplines, and religions. There's not a universally accepted “best” or “most effective” type; rather, it is our individual preference that helps us choose the one (or ones) that works best for us. Here's a breakdown of some of the more popular types of meditation to get you started.

Breath-focused

Breath-focused Meditation

This form of meditation is fairly straightforward because it uses the object of our breath to focus attention, to anchor the mind and maintain awareness. Notice your mind starting to wander? Simply return to the breath.

Body Scan

Body Scan Meditation

This form of meditation is fairly straightforward because it uses the object of our breath to focus attention, to anchor the mind and maintain awareness. Notice your mind starting to wander? Simply return to the breath.

Visualization

Visualization Meditation

This type of meditation invites you to picture something or someone in your mind — we are essentially replacing the breath with a mental image as the object of focus. It can feel challenging to some, but it's really no different than vividly recalling the face of an old friend naturally, without effort. And so it is with meditation. By conjuring a specific visualization, we not only get to observe the mind, but we also get to focus on any physical sensations.

Mantra

Mantra Meditation

This technique is similar to focused attention meditation, although instead of focusing on the breath to quiet the mind, you focus on a mantra (which could be a syllable, word, or phrase). The idea here is that the subtle vibrations associated with the repeated mantra can encourage positive change — maybe a boost in self-confidence or increased compassion for others — and help you enter an even deeper state of meditation.

How to Get Started

So you've decided to start looking after the health of your mind by meditating. The beginning is always the hardest part but there are many guided-meditation tools that you can use to help you. Some of my favourites are Calm, Headspace, Youtube videos and podcasts, or even books if you want to learn more about meditation before getting started. I recommend The Surrender Experiment by Michael A. Singer - it's personally what got me into meditation and it taught me a lot about being in the present moment.
